About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Northwestern University (expected May 2027), advised by Prof. Sinan Keten. My research sits at the intersection of theoretical and computational nanomechanics, focusing on the structure-property relationships of polymer-based nanocomposites and soft materials.

I apply tools from molecular dynamics simulation, machine learning, and continuum mechanics to understand how nanoscale architecture governs macroscopic performance — with applications in aerospace materials, gas-separation membranes, and tough hydrogels.

I am passionate about academic research and mentoring the next generation of engineers. I hold an EIT certification in Nebraska and bring both hands-on engineering and deep computational expertise.
