Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Northwestern University, where I am advised by Prof. Sinan Keten. I specialize in the theoretical and applied nanomechanics of engineering materials, with interest in polymer-grafted nanoparticle systems, hydrogels, gas-separation membranes, and biological and bio-inspired materials

My research combines large-scale molecular dynamics simulations, machine learning (including deep potential molecular dynamics), and continuum mechanics to unlock fundamental structure-property relationships in soft matter and nanocomposites. I aim to advance applications in aerospace, renewable energy, public health, and sustainable infrastructure.

I earned my B.S. in Civil and Environmental Engineering from the University of Nebraska–Lincoln (2022), where I graduated with High Distinction and earned the Engineer in Training (EIT) certification. Prior to Northwestern, I conducted undergraduate research on bio-inspired roadside safety hardware and post-disaster structural reconnaissance using digital data sources.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to mentoring and teaching the next generation of engineers. I enjoy the challenge of translating cutting-edge nanoscale science into practical engineering solutions.
