Teaching & Mentorship

I believe that clear communication of complex ideas is just as important as the research itself. My experience across simulation-heavy and experimentally collaborative projects has given me a broad foundation for mentoring students at the intersection of civil engineering, physics, and computational science.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe effective teaching means meeting students where they are — building intuition before formalism, connecting abstract concepts to real physical behavior, and creating space for questions that feel "too basic" but often unlock the deepest understanding.

My research spans both atomistic simulation and continuum mechanics theory, which means I routinely translate between scales and frameworks. That cross-disciplinary perspective informs how I explain topics: always with an eye toward why a concept matters and how it connects to the bigger picture.

Research Mentorship

As a graduate researcher in the Keten Lab at Northwestern, I work closely with undergraduate and junior graduate students on simulation setup, data analysis, and scientific writing. Mentorship is an integral part of graduate research culture in my group.

I take an active role in onboarding new students to tools like LAMMPS, Python-based analysis pipelines, HPC cluster workflows, and visualization software (Ovito, VMD). I aim to build independence, not just proficiency — so students understand the reasoning behind each step.

Topics I Can Teach or TA

Computational Materials Science

Molecular dynamics, force fields, statistical mechanics, coarse-graining, free energy methods.

Scientific Programming

Python for data analysis (NumPy, SciPy, Pandas, Matplotlib), MATLAB, HPC scripting, Git workflows.

Machine Learning for Science

Deep learning fundamentals, neural network potentials (DeePMD), ML-driven coarse-graining, regression and classification for materials data.

Structural Mechanics

Statics, mechanics of materials, fracture mechanics, continuum mechanics fundamentals.

Remote Sensing & Geospatial Data

NOAA/USGS satellite data processing, digital reconnaissance methodologies, geospatial analysis for infrastructure applications.

Scientific Writing & Communication

Journal article structure, abstract writing, conference abstracts and talks, effective technical figures and data visualization.
